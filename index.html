Learn the Thai Alphabet — Online Flashcard Trainer

ThaiLetters.com is a free, browser-based way to learn the Thai alphabet: all 44 consonants, the vowel signs, and the Thai numerals. Every flashcard pairs the letter with its traditional name, a plain-English pronunciation clue, its consonant class, and spoken audio — so you can start reading Thai letters with confidence. There is no signup and nothing to install, and your practice progress stays on your own device.

How the Thai alphabet trainer works

  1. Pick a set — consonants, vowels, numbers, or all of them together.
  2. See the letter and try to recall its name and sound from memory.
  3. Reveal the answer with a click or the Space bar to check the name, romanization, "sounds like" clue, and consonant class.
  4. Hear it read aloud with the speaker button (or press S).
  5. Grade yourself with the arrow keys, then drill the ones you missed at the end of each round.

What you can practice

The trainer covers the full everyday Thai writing system, split into focused decks so you can build up one piece at a time:

  • 44 consonants — each shown with its acrophonic name (for example, ก ไก่, "gor gai", the chicken) and its high, mid, or low class.
  • Vowel signs — short and long vowels and common combinations, written around a placeholder so you can see where they attach.
  • Thai numerals — the digits ๐ ๑ ๒ ๓ ๔ ๕ ๖ ๗ ๘ ๙ (0–9), still used on signs, menus, and official documents.

Thai consonant classes, explained

Every Thai consonant belongs to one of three classes — high, mid, or low. The class matters because, together with vowel length and any tone mark, it determines the tone of a syllable. Learning each letter's class early makes reading tones far easier later on.

How many letters are in the Thai alphabet?

The Thai alphabet has 44 consonant letters (two of which are now obsolete) plus a set of vowel symbols and four tone marks. Vowels are written with around 15 basic signs that combine into roughly 30 written forms, appearing before, after, above, or below the consonant they modify. Thai also has its own set of ten numerals. Unlike English, Thai is normally written without spaces between words, which is part of why recognizing each letter on sight is such a useful first step.

Tips for memorizing Thai letters

  • Practice in short, daily sessions rather than long, occasional ones.
  • Say each letter's full name out loud — the acrophonic word (like "gor gai") anchors the sound in memory.
  • Use the "sounds like" clue to tie each Thai sound to an English word you already know.
  • Learn each consonant's class as you go, so tone rules feel natural later.
  • At the end of every round, use "Drill the misses" to focus on the letters that need work.

Frequently asked questions

Is ThaiLetters.com free to use?

Yes. The trainer is completely free and runs entirely in your browser. There is no account to create and nothing to download; the site is supported by optional advertising.

Can I hear the Thai letters pronounced?

Yes. After you reveal a card, press the "Hear it" button or the S key to hear the letter's name read aloud. Audio uses your device's built-in speech voices; a Thai-language voice gives the most accurate result.

What are Thai consonant classes?

Thai consonants are grouped into high, mid, and low classes. The class of the initial consonant combines with vowel length and tone marks to decide a syllable's tone, so it is an essential part of reading Thai correctly.

Do I need to sign up or install anything?

No. ThaiLetters.com works instantly in any modern browser on desktop or mobile, with no sign-up and no installation.

How long does it take to learn the Thai alphabet?

It varies by learner, but with focused daily practice many people recognize all 44 consonants within a few weeks. Spaced, repeated review — exactly what flashcards provide — is the most reliable approach.
